About Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a conservation project aimed at protecting and preserving sea turtles in Sri Lanka. It is located in the coastal town of Kosgoda and has been operating for several years.

The hatchery plays a crucial role in increasing the survival rate of turtle hatchlings by collecting and protecting turtle eggs from predators and poachers. Once the eggs hatch, the baby turtles are released into the sea.

Turtle Species

The hatchery primarily focuses on five species of sea turtles:

  • Green Turtles
  • Hawksbill Turtles
  • Olive Ridley Turtles
  • Leatherback Turtles
  • Loggerhead Turtles

Conservation Efforts

In addition to protecting turtle eggs and releasing hatchlings, the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery also carries out various conservation efforts:

  • Rescue and rehabilitation of injured and sick turtles
  • Public awareness campaigns about sea turtle conservation
  • Research and monitoring of turtle populations
  • Collaboration with local communities, organizations, and government agencies

Visiting the Hatchery

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery welcomes visitors who are interested in learning about sea turtles and supporting their conservation. Visitors can:

  • Take guided tours to learn about the hatchery's operations
  • Observe turtle hatchlings and larger turtles in the tanks
  • Participate in releasing baby turtles into the ocean
  • Support the hatchery through donations

Visiting hours and entry fees may vary, so it's recommended to check the hatchery's official website or contact them for the most up-to-date information.
