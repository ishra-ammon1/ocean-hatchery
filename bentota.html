About Bentota Turtle Hatchery

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is a conservation project dedicated to the protection and preservation of sea turtles in Sri Lanka. Located in the coastal town of Bentota, the hatchery has been actively involved in turtle conservation for many years.

Turtle Conservation Efforts

The hatchery focuses on various conservation efforts to ensure the survival and well-being of sea turtles:

  • Protection of turtle eggs from natural threats and poaching
  • Caring for and raising hatchlings in a controlled environment
  • Releasing healthy turtles into the sea
  • Rescue and rehabilitation of injured turtles
  • Public education and awareness programs on sea turtle conservation

Turtle Species

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is home to several species of sea turtles, including:

  • Green Turtles
  • Hawksbill Turtles
  • Olive Ridley Turtles
  • Loggerhead Turtles

Visiting the Hatchery

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery welcomes visitors who are interested in learning about sea turtles and supporting their conservation. Visitors can:

  • Take guided tours to observe and learn about the turtles
  • Witness the release of hatchlings into the ocean
  • Participate in turtle feeding sessions
  • Support the hatchery through donations
