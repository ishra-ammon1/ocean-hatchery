Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, located on the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka, is a renowned conservation facility dedicated to protecting and preserving sea turtles. Nestled amidst pristine beaches and vibrant marine life, the hatchery serves as a sanctuary for these magnificent creatures. The dedicated staff at the hatchery work tirelessly to ensure the safety of sea turtle eggs, providing a nurturing environment for their hatching and subsequent release into the ocean. Visitors to the hatchery have the opportunity to witness the remarkable life cycle of sea turtles, from the moment they are hatched to their eventual return to the sea. Through educational programs and guided tours, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery aims to raise awareness about the importance of sea turtle conservation, inspiring visitors to actively participate in safeguarding these endangered species.


The hatchery focuses on five species of sea turtles that are found in Sri Lankan waters, including the Olive Ridley, Green, Loggerhead, Hawksbill, and Leatherback turtles. The dedicated team at the hatchery collects sea turtle eggs that are laid on nearby beaches, protecting them from natural threats such as predators and human activities. These eggs are then carefully incubated in a controlled environment to enhance their survival rate.


Once the baby turtles hatch, visitors have the unique opportunity to witness the captivating sight of hundreds of tiny turtles making their way towards the ocean. It's a heartwarming experience to observe the hatchlings embark on their perilous journey, guided by their instincts and the shimmering moonlight.


In addition to hatching and releasing sea turtles, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery also takes in injured or sick turtles for rehabilitation. The facility provides medical care and nursing to these injured turtles, ensuring their recovery before they are released back into their natural habitat.


The hatchery also plays a crucial role in educating the public about sea turtle conservation. Knowledgeable staff members provide guided tours and interactive programs, where visitors can learn about the different species of sea turtles, their life cycles, threats they face, and how they can contribute to their protection. By raising awareness and fostering a sense of responsibility, the hatchery aims to create a lasting impact on marine conservation efforts.


The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery serves as a beacon of hope for the survival of sea turtles in Sri Lanka. It combines scientific research, conservation practices, and public engagement to ensure the long-term viability of these beautiful creatures. Through their tireless efforts, the hatchery is making a significant contribution to the preservation of marine biodiversity and the overall health of the oceans.
